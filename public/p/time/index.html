Here at Baruffio we sometimes cast a critical eye on Harry Potter, and amuse ourselves by pointing out inconsistencies in the books (then weaving them into conspiracy theories). But time travel in Prisoner of Azkaban is a model of consistency, illustrating wonderfully how time travel works—if it’s possible at all.

The past is past

Dumbledore held up his hand to stem the flood of explanations.

‘It is your turn to listen, and I beg you will not interrupt me, because there is very little time,’ he said quietly. ‘There is not a shred of proof to support Black’s story, except your word—and the word of two thirteen-year-old wizards will not convince anybody. A street full of eye-witnesses swore they saw Sirius murder Pettigrew. I myself gave evidence to the Ministry that Sirius had been the Potters’ Secret Keeper.”

“Professor Lupin can tell you—” Harry said, unable to stop himself.

“Professor Lupin is currently deep in the Forest, unable to tell anyone anything. By the time he is human again, it will be too late, Sirius will be worse than dead. I might add that werewolves are so mistrusted by most of our kind that his support will count for very little—and the fact that he and Sirius are old friends—”

“But—”

“Listen to me, Harry. It is too late, you understand me?”

Just so everyone’s on the same page, let’s review the events that lead up to this conversation. Hermione finds Scabbers the rat in Hagrid’s cabin, and she, Harry, Ron, and Scabbers leave before the execution of Buckbeat the Hippogriff is scheduled to take place—at sunset. Sirius Black, in his Animagus form of a dog, drags Ron into the secret passage under the Whomping Willow, and Ron takes Scabbers with him. Crookshanks hits a switch at the base of the Willow and freezes it, allowing him and Harry and Hermione to follow Sirius, Ron, and Scabbers.

Lupin follows them into the Shrieking Shack, and then Snape comes soon after—maybe 20 minutes after sunset. He finds the Invisibility Cloak that Harry dropped and enters the Shrieking Shack, but is stunned and never sees Pettigrew. Lupin convinces Harry, Ron, and Hermione of Sirius’ innocence; Pettigrew is revealed and forced to admit that he, not Sirius, betrayed Harry’s parents to Voldemort. Then the whole party exists the Whomping Willow, intending to take Pettigrew up to Hogwarts.

But the full moon is out. Lupin transforms into a werewolf, Pettigrew escapes, and Harry, Hermione, and Sirius are surrounded by Dementors and fall unconscious. When Harry and Hermione wake up in the hospital wing, Sirius is locked in a room upstairs and Cornelius Fudge has ordered that he recieve the Dementor’s Kiss.
